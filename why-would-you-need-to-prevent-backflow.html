FAQS

What is backflow and why is it a concern?

Backflow is the unwanted reversal of water flow in a plumbing system, which can lead to contamination of the water supply. It is a concern because it can pose serious health risks if contaminated water enters the clean water supply.

How does backflow prevention benefit me as a homeowner?

Backflow prevention ensures that the water you use for drinking, cooking, and bathing remains clean and safe. By preventing backflow, you are safeguarding the health of your family and maintaining the integrity of your plumbing system.

Is backflow prevention required by law in Australia?

Yes, in most states and territories in Australia, backflow prevention measures are mandatory for certain types of properties, such as commercial buildings, medical facilities, and properties with irrigation systems. It is important to check with your local authorities to determine the specific requirements in your area.

How often should backflow prevention devices be tested?

Backflow prevention devices should be tested at least once a year to ensure they are functioning correctly and effectively preventing backflow. Regular testing and maintenance are essential to keep the devices in optimal condition and comply with regulations.

Can I install a backflow prevention device myself, or should I hire a professional?

It is recommended to hire a professional to install and test backflow prevention devices. A qualified backflow specialist will have the knowledge and expertise to ensure that the device is installed correctly and meets regulatory requirements. It is important to choose a reputable professional to ensure the safety and effectiveness of the backflow prevention system.
